One-shot Voice Conversion by Disentangling Representations with Domain Seperation Networks

Siyi Zhou, Minchuan Chen, Jun Ma, Shaojun Wang, Jing Xiao

Ping An Technology, China

Abstract

Most previous works on voice conversion (VC) require either parallel corpora or sufficient training data from target speakers. In this paper, we propose a Domain Separation Network based VC (DSN-VC) system, which can perform VC from an arbitrary source speaker to an arbitrary target speaker or even source speaker and target speaker are not seen in the training set. In our proposed model, a speaker encoder is utilized to capture speaker-specific representations from the input acoustic features, and a content encoder is trained to capture speaker-independent content representations by leveraging a gradient reversal layer (GRL) and a speaker classifier. Meanwhile, the content and speaker representations are further pushed apart with soft orthogonality constraint. A phoneme recognition module is introduced to map the content representations to phonetic posteriorgrams (PPGs). Experimental results demonstrate that the proposed DSN-VC model achieves better results compared to the baseline.
